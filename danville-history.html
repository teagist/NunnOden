Danville Lodge No. 95

Preserving the Legacy of Our Brethren

In Memoriam

Danville Lodge No. 95 served its community with distinction for many years. Upon its closing in 2010 its members and legacy became part of Nunn-Oden Lodge No. 398.

A Brief History

Formation: Danville Lodge was originally organized under the name of Oakville Lodge No. 95 and received their dispensation on October 16th, 1848.

Names on Petitions dated July 22, 1848:

Darius Lynch H. Byars
A.P. Rainwater Pleasant Sivley
R. Byars James M. Lepper

Names on Dispensation: The above names, plus:

Junior Warden: Edward Bracken
Senior Warden: Lewis H. Sadler
Worshipful Master: David M. Daney

Dispensation continued by the Grand Lodge for twelve months-December 7, 1848. On December 6, 1849 a Charter was granted to Oakville Lodge No. 95 located in Oakville Alabama.

Relocation & Renaming: Oakville Lodge No. 95 moved to Danville, Alabama between the months of October and December 1859. The Lodge was located near the College at Danville. On December 6, 1859 the Grand Lodge secretary offered a resolution to change the name of Oakville Lodge No. 95 to Danville Lodge No. 95, and it was adopted.

Final Years: From 1859 to 2010, Danville Lodge continued to serve their community and the larger Masonic district.
