Nunn-Oden Lodge No. 398

Our History

A Legacy of Brotherhood, Service, and Tradition

Introduction

Since its founding, Nunn-Oden Lodge No. 398 has stood as a beacon of Freemasonry in Hartselle, Alabama. Through generations of faithful service, the lodge has upheld the timeless principles of Brotherly Love, Relief, and Truth.

Key Milestones

1875
Chartering of the Lodge

Hartselle Lodge No. 398, Under Dispensation, was approved to receive a Charter, marking the beginning of a long tradition of Masonry in the Hartselle community.

1876-1910
Early Growth and Community Presence

In its early years, the lodge quickly became a central part of the community, drawing members committed to moral improvement and service.

1914
Construction of the Lodge Building

The lodge established its physical home, creating a lasting space for fellowship, ritual, and community engagement.

1971
Renaming to Honor Distinguished Service

Brother James Eidson, Sr., presented a proposal to the Grand Lodge of Alabama to change the Lodge name to Nunn-Oden Lodge, No. 398 to honor the legacy and distinguished service of the Honorable Brother Troy L. Nunn, PGM, and longstanding Secretary, Brother E. A. Oden, PM.

2018
Consolidation with Danville Lodge No. 86

Danville Lodge No. 86 consolidated some of its members with Nunn-Oden Lodge and Falkville Lodge No. 396.

Read more about the history of Danville Lodge No. 86

2026
Modern Day

Today, Nunn-Oden Lodge remains active and vibrant, continuing its mission while embracing the future.
